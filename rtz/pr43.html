RTZ-CRA and Freeport -McMoRan Finalise Exploration and Expansion Joint Ventures


The RTZ-CRA Group and Freeport-McMoRan Copper & Gold (FCX) today signed definitive documentation of previously announced agreements in principle for exploration and expansion joint ventures. As a result, RTZ-CRA has acquired from FCX's Indonesian mining subsidiary, PT Freeport Indonesia Company (PTFI), a 40% interest in all future production expansions above 118,000 tonnes of ore per day under PTFI's contract of work.

RTZ-CRA and FCX and its Indonesian subsidiaries agreed in principle in May 1995 to establish joint venture programmes. Pursuant to the definitive documentation signed today, RTZ-CRA has fulfilled its commitment to fund $100 million of exploration expenses. Once this amount has been fully expended subsequent exploration expenditure will be shared 60 per cent by FCX's subsidiaries and 40 per cent by RTZ-CRA. RTZ-CRA has also contributed $84m of expansion costs. This comprises both its own 40% share of costs to date and an advance to PTFI under a $450 million non-recourse loan towards PTFI's share of costs for expansion activities above 118,000 tonnes of ore per day in PTFI's Block A area, where current mining and milling operations are underway.

Once the expansion is completed, RTZ-CRA will receive 100 per cent of incremental cash flows from the expanded operations above 118,000 tonnes of ore per day until RTZ-CRA recoups the amount loaned to PTFI. Subsequently, cash flows from the expanded operations will be shared 60 per cent by PTFI and 40 per cent by RTZ-CRA. FCX will retain 100 per cent of the production and cash flows associated with the mill throughput up to 118,000 tonnes per day.

As previously announced, RTZ-CRA and FCX have established the economic justification for an expansion of PTFI's facilities, subject to approval of the relevant Indonesian authorities. Engineering activities have begun on the expansion of PTFI's Indonesian operations which are currently producing at a throughput rate of 125,000 tonnes of ore per day. The optimum rate of this "fourth concentrator mill expansion" is expected to be at least 190,000 to 200,000 tonnes of ore per day and completion is expected in the second half of 1998. Costs for this expansion to achieve optimum throughput rates are expected to approximate $960 million, including $300 million to construct a coal fired power plant and related facilities.
