Events

 

The Gaudy is the main activity of the year. Held at College, its core is lunch, the AGM and afternoon tea, a discussion on important issues for the College and may include a concert in the Jacqueline du Pré Music Building. In alternate years, a Gaudy dinner usually rounds off the evening. The weekend can continue to include a Sunday morning seminar on topical issues. The next Gaudy is on 23/24 September 2000.

 

Two meetings are held in London every year, the Autumn Drinks Party, which is held on a Thursday evening in November for people working in and around London and Senior Members who might be able to make a trip to London. The next one will be on 9 November 2000. The other London event is the long-standing London Tea Party Meeting which combines an interesting talk, followed by a relaxed get together over afternoon tea. Recent talks have included P.D. James on detective story writing and the TV news reporter Sue Lloyd-Roberts. The next London meeting is on 23 February 2000 at 3pm at St Alban's Centre, Baldwin's Gardens, London EC1, when the speaker will be Professor Susan Greenfield (1970-73).

 

Other events, arranged from time to time in reponse to the wishes and suggestions of members, include subject/profession conferences and seminars, literary lunches and dinners and purely social gatherings.

 

Some events eg. the Gaudy Dinners and special conferences, are restricted to Senior Members, but families and friends are always welcome at many events such as lunches, garden parties, concerts,

 

Reunions

 

The ASM and the College encourage reunions, usually taken for the year of coming up, i.e. matriculation, or by subject. Fiftieth, twenty-fifth and decade anniversary lunches or dinners have proved the most popular to date. The Office will help with all the arrangements, from the mailing to the meal and will be very pleased to assist if you wish to initiate a reunion.

 

Also encouraged and organized by the Office is a reunion lunch for those who have just become eligible to receive the M.A.
