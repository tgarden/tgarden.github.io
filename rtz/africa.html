Africa

RTZ-CRA's operations in Africa account for 5 per cent of assets, 9 per cent of net attributable earnings, and employ around 9,000. Principal operations are: the Palabora copper mine (39 per cent) and the Richards Bay Minerals (50 per cent) titanium dioxide feedstock producer, in South Africa, Rossing Uranium (68 per cent) in Namibia, and Rio Tinto Zimbabwe (56 per cent). There is currently exploration in a number of sub Saharan countries.

Palabora (38.9 per cent)

Palabora Mining Company, which is managed by RTZ-CRA, operates a large low grade open pit copper mine, smelter and refinery in the north east Transvaal. The deposit was identified in the early 1950s and a joint venture company was formed in 1956 to develop the mine. Palabora is a listed South African company and one of the Republic's largest copper producers. The mine started up in 1964 and accounts for 75 per cent of South African primary copper product sales. Palabora's total sales represent 2.4 per cent of South Africa's total mineral output.

Reserves are sufficient to continue open pit production until 2002. Thereafter a new undergound mine will extend the life of the mine by at least 20 vears. Ore reserves below the existing open pit will be worked and the material processed through the existing metallurgical plant. Planned annual production is 90,000 tonnes of copper.

About half the refined copper is made into continuous cast rod for sale to South African fabricators, with the remainder exported as cathodes. Excess production above fixed contracts is sold on the spot market.

The by-products from the copper process include zirconium chemicals, uranium oxide, magnetite and nickel sulphate, as well as small quantities of gold, silver and platinum. In addition, sulphuric acid is produced as a by- product from the smelter. The company also owns an adjacent vermiculite deposit which is mined and concentrated for sale worldwide.
The Palabora mine is among the largest open pit copper mining operations in the world

Richards Bay (50 per cent)

At Richards Bay dredge ponds are created in the ore bearing dunes and the minerals are removed by pumping sand and water through gravity separation equipment

 

Pioneering mining techniquesfrom Australia and sophisticated Canadian smelting technology are the basis of Richards Bay Minerals, formed in 1976 to mine, process and market the heavy minerals which occur in the sand dunes of the Zululand coast. RTZ-CRA has a 50 per cent interest with South African interests owning the remainder.

RBM is located north of Durban in Natal. The company mines heavy minerals from the dunes by suction dredges floating on artificial ponds. Gravity, magnetic and electrostatic processes are used to extract the economic components of ilmenite, zircon, rutile and monazite. The latter three products are sold in their natural form and the ilmenite is upgraded and smelted to produce an 85 per cent titanium dioxide feedstock and pig iron.

Titanium dioxide feedstock is RBM's major product. It is a non toxic compound with hundreds of uses in dailv life and an essential raw material for the production of white pigment used worldwide in the manufacture of paints, plastics and papers.

The ilmenite at Richards Bay has a low alkali content, which makes the product suitable for the more environmentally acceptable chloride process of producing titanium dioxide pigment.

Smelting produces titanium dioxide feedstock and high purity iron

Rössing Uranium (68 per cent)

RTZ-CRA manages Rossing Uranium, an open pit uranium mine and processing plant in the Namib Desert, Namibia. The mine started production in 1976 and is one of the world's most efficient open pit uranium mines.

Uranium ore is extracted from the open pit and processed on site into uranium oxide. The uranium oxide is exported for use solely by electricity generating utilities worldwide. The sales contracts are subject to rigorous national and international safeguards

The mine and plant were designed to produce 5,000 short tons of uranium oxide per annum. However, the depressed uranium market made a significant contraction of operations necessary in 1991 and annual output is now some 2,300 tonnes of uranium oxide(U3O8). The work force is now about 1,300 people. Despite these reductions it is estimated that Rossing still accounts for about 7 per cent of Namibia's GDP, 12 per cent of exports and 4 per cent of total tax revenue.

Rossing, in the heart of the Namib desert,accounts for 12 per cent of that country's exports

Rio Tinto Zimbabwe (56 per cent)

Rio Tinto Zimbabwe is mainly involved in gold mining at Renco, Patchway and Brompton mines, waste dump retreatment at Eiffel Flats, and in nickel refining. Renco, which opened in 1982, is Zimbabwe's largest single gold mine and Rio Tinto Zimbabwe accounts for some 15 per cent of the country's arnual gold production. Empress Nickel toll refines for customers.
