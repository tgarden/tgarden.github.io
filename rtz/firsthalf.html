12 SEPTEMBER 1996

 

RTZ-CRA 1996 FIRST HALF RESULTS

RTZ-CRA's first half 1996 sales volume gains were more than offset by lower metal prices, adverse exchange rate effects and US copper smelter production delays.

Group profit before tax and exceptional items was $891m (1995: $1,253m) and adjusted earnings were $552m (1995: $711m) for the first six months of 1996.

Lower quoted metal prices offset other price improvements, reducing earnings by $194m, while exchange rate effects accounted for a further $34m.

Reduced earnings were reported by Kennecott (down 66%) reflecting not only price reductions but production build up delays at its new copper smelter. Australian dollar strength affected local operations while Comalco (down 58%) also suffered lower aluminium prices and Hamersley Iron (down 19%) lower sales.

Higher sales volumes elsewhere raised earnings by $39m. Production volumes rose : aluminium 3%; copper 17%; gold 18%, iron ore 9%, and titanium dioxide feedstock 16%.

Earnings growth areas included industrial minerals, with Iron & Titanium up 62%, Borax 10%, and Argyle Diamonds 30%, as well as the expanded Escondida copper mine, up 28%.

Major development programme progressing well. Five projects commissioning in the next 18 months. Global exploration restructuring and refocusing complete.

Mr Robert Wilson, chief executive of RTZ-CRA, said: "There were notable first half improvements in RTZ-CRA's worldwide industrial minerals businesses and at the expanded Escondida copper mine. However, Group results were impacted by lower quoted metal prices, adverse exchange rate movements, the slow build up to full production at Kennecott's new copper smelter and labour problems at our Australian coal operations.

"First-half demand for metals in the large industrial economies has been mixed. Western world copper consumption was over 3 per cent higher than in the first half of 1995 but demand for some others was down on the previous year, including aluminium by almost 7 per cent. Overall, non-ferrous metal prices declined by 11 per cent compared with the first half of 1995.

"Our programme of major development projects is progressing well. The most advanced include: $114 million Greens Creek (US) silver/zinc (now commissioning); $319 million Pipeline (US) gold (mid 1997); $750 million Boyne Island (Australia) aluminium (mid 1997); $260 million QIT (Canada) UGS plant (late 1997); and $233 million Fortaleza (Brazil) nickel (early 1998).

"Exploration results continue to be promising, especially in Canadian diamonds and Indonesian copper/gold. Greater efficiencies following the refocusing and restructuring of global exploration are expected to show savings of $50 million per year from 1997."

Sir Derek Birkin, RTZ's chairman said: "Market sentiment has had a sharp adverse impact on metal prices in 1996. Confidence was shaken by Sumitomo's announcement in June. The current price levels of many metals appear to be more depressed than the fundamentals of supply, demand and stocks would suggest.

"Economic indicators point to a broadly based improvement in the second half of this year and stronger markets in 1997. On a longer-term view, we are confident about growth of demand, especially from the Asian region which now accounts for a significant share of global consumption of all metals and minerals.

"For RTZ-CRA, with production of our major products still increasing and with our strong financial position, the longer term holds considerable promise."

1996 HALF YEAR RESULTS

A decline in average metal prices and unfavourable exchange rates coincided with reduced iron ore sales from Australia to Japan and Europe and difficulties at the new US copper smelter. The falling copper price also resulted in adjustments to the provisional pricing of sales to smelters on deferred pricing terms.

Group sales revenue at $4,003 million was only 6 per cent lower as generally higher volumes and bulk commodity and industrial mineral prices partially offset lower quoted metal prices.

Profit before tax and exceptional items at $891 million was 29 per cent lower. The tax rate is lower at 30 per cent in 1996 partly reflecting the absence of the effect of the introduction of a higher corporate tax rate in Australia in the first half of 1995.



Operating cash flow at $881 million was down by 29 per cent, the two main reasons being lower prices and the effects of the smelter delay. Higher capital expenditure at $756 million reflected the Group's substantial development programme.

Balance Sheet

Net debt increased to $1.9 billion in the first half of 1996 and as a percentage of total capital it rose to 18.8 per cent compared with 16.1 per cent at the start of the year. Net interest expense (including capitalised interest of $29 million) was covered 24 times by pre-interest profits. Retained profits and the strength of the Australian dollar increased shareholders' funds by $445 million.

Dividends

Interim dividends equivalent to 16.5 US cents per share have been declared by RTZ and CRA, marginally higher than the US dollar equivalent of the 1995 RTZ interim dividend. As announced in May 1996, the balance between interim and final dividends will follow the former RTZ practice which historically has meant that approximately one third of the annual total has been paid at the half year and two thirds at the full year.

RTZ-CRA's policy aims to increase dividends progressively over time, without cutting dividends in economic downturns. The US dollar, in which the majority of sales are made, most reliably records the Group's global business performance and is now RTZ-CRA's main reporting currency. It is, therefore, the natural currency in which to determine dividends. However, CRA dividends will still be declared and paid in Australian dollars and RTZ dividends declared and paid in pounds sterling, converted at the exchange rates applicable two days prior to announcement of dividends.

Interim dividends will be paid of 10.60 pence per ordinary share (1995: 10.50p) as a Foreign Income Dividend (FID) to RTZ shareholders and of 20.65 Australian cents per ordinary share (1995: 30 cents) as a fully franked dividend to CRA shareholders. Dividend payments to RTZ and CRA ADR holders will be made by the Bank of New York at the rate of 66.0 US cents per ADR.

The respective dividends will be paid on 21 October 1996 to holders registered at close of business on 1 October 1996, and to RTZ bearer shareholders on or after 21 October 1996, against coupon 75. The ex-dividend date will be 23 September 1996. Dividends to RTZ and CRA ADR holders will be paid on 22 October.

Dividends of 1.6625 pence per RTZ 'A' preference share and 1.75 pence per 'B' preference share for the second half of 1996 will be paid to holders registered on 19 November 1996, on 2 January, 1997, and to 'B' preference bearer shareholders on or after that date against coupon 69. The ex-dividend date will be 11 November, 1996.


REVIEW OF OPERATIONS

Copper and gold 1996 first half earnings $190m, down 46%

 

Copper Production up 17% to 363,000 tonnes

Reported copper market stocks remain in reasonable balance but the average copper price in the first half of 1996 fell to 115 per pound from 132 per pound a year earlier.

RTZ-CRA share of output at Escondida (Chile) was up 84 per cent to 123,300 tonnes as the Phase 3 expansion came on stream. Grasberg's (Indonesia) production increased by 26 per cent following mine and mill expansions completed at the end of 1995. Mine production at Palabora (South Africa) was also up by 6 per cent as a result of mineralogy and process improvements.

Production at the Bingham Canyon mine (US) fell by 11 per cent as favourable recovery and throughput only partially offset temporarily lower ore grades. Neves Corvo (Portugal) production also fell by 10 per cent due to planned mining of lower grade ore.

Overall, RTZ-CRA's refined copper production was reduced by 28 per cent mainly due to production build-up delays at Kennecott's smelter in the US.

Gold Production up 18% to 905,000 ounces

The average gold price improved by 3 per cent to $395 per ounce.

RTZ-CRA gold production was boosted by a 79,000 ounce half year contribution from the Grasberg mine. Production was higher at most other Group mines, including Bingham Canyon (up 12 per cent), Kelian (Indonesia - up 12 per cent), Peak (Australia - up 25 per cent) and Ridgeway (US - up 16 per cent). Improvements resulted from higher gold grades and improved throughput.


Iron ore 1996 first half earnings $103m, down 18%
Production up 9% to 26.5m tonnes

RTZ-CRA share of first half production was at record levels. The stronger Australian dollar offset the average 6 per cent increase in Hamersley Iron's prices for deliveries in 1996/97. Demand was down in Europe and Japan but firm elsewhere in Asia, with shipments to China at record levels. The net effect, however, was that overall sales volumes were down 9 per cent.


Coal 1996 first half earnings $79m, down 6%

Australian and Indonesian coal Production down 5% to 11.9m tonnes

RTZ-CRA's coal mines in Australia were affected by operational problems and industrial relations disputes. Sales were consequently down 16 per cent at Blair Athol and Vickery suffered a significant loss of customers. Coal & Allied reported a loss in the first half with Hunter Valley production reduced by 14 per cent following lower output from the coal preparation plant, which is currently being upgraded. Total sales volumes for Coal & Allied were down 15 per cent.

In Indonesia, output at Kaltim Prima was higher and sales volumes increased by 14 per cent. Opportunities to increase market outlets for Kaltim Prima coal are under investigation with particular focus on supply to the growing independent power sector in Asia.


US coal Production up 2% to 20.1m tonnes

Responding to both new and renewed contracts, output and sales from RTZ-CRA's US coal operations increased, partially offsetting weak spot prices. In the Powder River Basin, the decline in prices for coal from Cordero slowed while prices for coal from Spring Creek and Antelope stabilised. At Colowyo in Colorado, RTZ-CRA production increased by 27 per cent to 2.6m tonnes.

Aluminium 1996 first half earnings $48m, down 56%
Bauxite production up 4% at 3.3m tonnes;
aluminium metal up 3% at 185,000 tonnes

Comalco's earnings declined significantly in the first half of 1996 due to unfavourable LME prices, the stronger Australian dollar and weaker alumina spot prices.

The diversion of resources from pre-development to mining at Weipa, Australia, resulted in higher bauxite output. The Tiwai Point, New Zealand, smelter upgrade is commissioning and construction work on the expansion of the Boyne Island smelter in Australia is ahead of schedule and expected to begin producing in mid-1997.

In the UK, Anglesey Aluminium's production was at record levels as a result of improved efficiencies, more cells operating and higher productivity.

Borates 1996 first half earnings $64m, up 10%
Production similar at 274,000 tonnes

Borate sales revenue was 5 per cent up on the first half of 1995 with higher overall volumes.
North American performance was affected by a poor agricultural season but this was offset by strong sales to the insulation fibreglass industry. European sales were higher overall than last year with gains in most applications other than detergents. The Asia Pacific market showed good volume gains. After several static years, prices generally improved.

Titanium dioxide feedstock 1996 first half earnings $73m, up 62%
Production up 16% to 726,000 tonnes

A general improvement in Iron & Titanium's business was complemented by significant price increases for two important co-products. Market tightness for both zircon and rutile resulted in substantial price increases compared with the first half of 1995.

Richards Bay Minerals in South Africa also gained from an 11 per cent weakening of the Rand against the US dollar and a reduction in South African tax rates.

Production at QIT in Canada increased primarily to meet slag inventory requirements at its Sorel smelter in advance of the start-up of the upgraded slag (UGS) plant in late 1997.

Other metals and minerals

Diamonds

First half sales of rough and polished diamonds were well above 1995 levels reflecting higher production (up 12 per cent) and strong demand for Argyle products which is expected to continue in the medium term. The existing European sales office in Antwerp, which has traditionally sold over 20 per cent of Argyle's diamonds, is being expanded to handle all sales following Argyle's decision not to renew its contract with the Central Selling Organisation. Most of Argyle's production will continue to be sold into India where strong customer relationships already exist.

Zinc, Lead, Silver

RTZ-CRA's share of zinc production, mainly from mines in Bolivia and Argentina, increased by 5 per cent to 19,300 tonnes. Total output of zinc metal was also higher by 5 per cent at 39,100 tonnes. Elsewhere, lead production was down 15 per cent while silver production rose 8 per cent, the latter reflecting higher mine output at Bingham Canyon and a higher contribution from Grasberg.

Salt

Output from Dampier Salt increased by 26 per cent and sales by 9 per cent. The market firmed towards the end of the first half and two significant supply agreements were negotiated with Asian customers.

Talc

Production of talc was unchanged at 554,000 tonnes. Inventory reductions in the paper industry reduced demand and bad weather impacted on paint sales.

Uranium

Productivity gains, improved throughput and higher grades increased output from the Rössing mine in Namibia by 54 per cent to 942 tonnes of U3O8. Spot prices continued to improve.


NEW PROJECT DEVELOPMENT UPDATE

RTZ-CRA's project development programme is progressing well. New production is already on stream at Escondida following the completion in May 1996 of the $560 million Phase 3 expansion. At the Tiwai Point (New Zealand) aluminium smelter the $325 million upgrade is virtually complete and, like the $114 million Green's Creek re-development in the US, is commissioning.

The $750 million expansion of the Boyne Island aluminium smelter in Australia is expected to start up in mid-1997 with 217,000 tonnes per year additional capacity.

Construction has begun at the Pipeline gold project in the US, where production is scheduled to commence in the first half of 1997. The neighbouring South Pipeline deposit has multi-million ounce potential and may enter the permitting phase in 1996.

Engineering work is over 90 per cent complete and construction 36 per cent complete at the $700 million Lihir Gold mine in Papua New Guinea while the $47 million Morro do Ouro gold mine expansion in Brazil is one third completed. Construction of the $233 million Fortaleza nickel mine in Brazil is on schedule for early 1998 start-up.

Site preparation and foundation work is well underway on QIT's $260 million development of the UGS process with its product being particularly attractive to the growing chloride sector of the pigment market. Commissioning is expected to commence in the third quarter 1997.

Shaft sinking and underground development began in June on the $440 million underground mine project to extend the life of the Palabora copper mine.

At Grasberg in Indonesia, a feasibility study is nearing completion on the expansion of the mine and mill facilities. Initially the expansion will be undertaken on the basis of the currently permitted 160,000 tonnes per day level. Comprehensive studies have been undertaken to determine the optimum level of operation above 160,000 tonnes per day. Operations at the higher level, which is currently expected to be at least 190,000 to 200,000 tonnes per day, will begin immediately after current studies are completed and government approvals are received. The project should be completed by mid 1998.

Two further expansion projects are under consideration at Escondida following the completion of Phase 3. An oxide project to produce 125,000 tonnes per annum of copper cathode is currently in final feasibility. In addition, various options will be evaluated by the end of 1997 for a Phase 4 expansion to compensate for future declining head grades.

All engineering development work on the Century Zinc project in Australia has ceased while the company negotiates with aboriginal claimants under the right-to-negotiate procedures of the Native Title Act.

EXPLORATION UPDATE

A review of RTZ-CRA's combined exploration has been completed and the Group is already benefiting from significant re-focusing on the most prospective targets worldwide. The restructuring has eliminated overlaps and cut back marginal programmes with reductions spread evenly across the major regions. Exploration in South America, Asia and Africa will largely be maintained. Cost savings in the remainder of 1996 will be offset by one-off re-structuring costs but, from 1997, savings in annual expenditure will be $50 million per year, when compared with 1995 expenditure.

Diamonds

Most of the underground bulk sample from A-154 South pipe in the North West Territories of Canada has been processed with encouraging results. The sample grade to date is approximately 4.6 carats per tonne, with the largest gem quality stone weighing 6.02 carats. The results support earlier indications of an inferred resource of 8.4 million tonnes down to 250 metres. Initial valuation suggests approximately $260 per tonne of kimberlite and compares favourably with operating mines around the world.

At the A-418 pipe, 700 metres southwest of A-154 South, mining of a 3,000 tonne bulk sample will be complete by the year end. Earlier in the year, a grade of 4.02 carats per tonne was indicated. Results from drilling on A-154 North indicated a grade of 2.19 carats per tonne.

Copper and Gold

Potential for a significant underground orebody adjacent to the Grasberg mine in Indonesia has been indicated by drilling results from the Amole Tunnel. Drill holes have returned intersections in excess of 100 metres with grades in the range of 2 per cent equivalent copper.

The Las Cruces prospect in Andalucia, southern Spain, continues to return promising results. The deposit contains a copper resource grading approximately 6 per cent copper, as well as polymetallic base and precious metal mineralisation. The total geological resource for all mineralisation types is in excess of 20 million tonnes at present. Approximately 140 holes have been drilled on the site to date and planning for an underground bulk sampling programme has commenced.

RTZ-CRA has contract of work applications on strategically well-sited land positions in Kalimantan, Indonesia, where there has been considerable exploration activity following the neighbouring discovery of a multi-million ounce gold deposit.
