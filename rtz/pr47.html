Comalco Announces Weipa Restructuring Plan

Comalco Limited has announced a major overhaul of its Weipa operations to improve its competitive position, enhance the long-term security of its bauxite mine and facilitate its proposed alumina refinery investment.

The restructuring plan includes:

  • A re-equipment programme, involving reduction and renewal of the mine's truck fleet, extension of the mine railway network and upgrading of the bauxite processing plant. This will enable the same volume of bauxite to be produced more effectively.
  • Rationalisation of the Brisbane and Weipa management of the operating business unit, Comalco Minerals and Alumina (CM&A). This will ultimately halve the management numbers.
  • Cessation of Weipa kaolin production. The substantial further investment required to achieve a sustainable market share cannot be justified by profitability projections. Continuing at the current production rate of 110,000 tonnes per year is not economically viable. People in 93 kaolin roles will be affected, although it is expected that a significant number will be placed within CM&A or other Group companies. The kaolin plant will be placed on a care and maintenance basis at this stage.

Subject to completion of estimates, Comalco Limited will incur an abnormal charge to 1996 earnings of up to A$40 million net after tax as a result of closing the kaolin business.


The proposed process improvements mean that Weipa will operate with a significantly smaller workforce in the future. To date 60 people have chosen voluntary redundancy packages as part of this restructuring programme.

CM&A's Managing Director, Rod Kinkead-Weekes, said that these changes would be staggered over the next two to three years. "They come as no surprise to our employees, who have been regularly informed of the need to make our Weipa business more competitive. My expectation is that there will not be many involuntary retrenchments. Our turnover rate is 60-70 people a year. We are taking every step possible to seek out opportunities for Group transfers and we are providing a generous and non-discriminatory redundancy package which will be fair to all employees," he said.
